What is Arthroscopy Surgery, it's symptoms and how physiotherapy can help in this regard?

Arthroscopy also referred as arthroscopic or keyhole surgery. This is an invasion surgery on a joint and most of time arthroscope is used for treatment. Arthroscopic surgery is a major surgical operation with less pain and fast recovery. Recovery time period is about 6 weeks. It requires proper post operation physiotherapy. Having an arthroscopic medical procedure to fix tendons and right joint tissues diminishes the danger of infection from open medical procedure and lessens the patient's recuperation time, however it doesn't dispense with the requirement for active recuperation therapy. Numerous patients accept that the solution for their knee, hip, shoulder, or elbow issue is to have a surgery. The most common joints that undergo arthroscopy include the knee, shoulder, ankle, hip and elbow, etc.



Symptoms of Arthroscopy Surgery:

  • Experiencing Pain
  • Loss of Motion
  • Redness in the joint
  • Swelling


How Physiotherapy helps to cure Arthroscopy Surgery:


Physiotherapy after arthroscopy is a basic piece of the treatment plan involving standard exercises to reestablish strength. In the pre-rehabilitation program, your physiotherapist will educate the patient about pain management and necessary exercises of 20 to 30 minutes to limit swelling and improve muscle strength. Active and passive knee flexion exercises are offered in the first two weeks by a physiotherapist to reduce pain and heal properly.

It is necessary to have extension exercises to allow the repaired organ to function properly. Hip adduction, bridging, and straight leg raises exercises are necessary to have a proper walk after surgery. In the rehabilitation program, the physiotherapist will allow intensive functional exercises by aquatic or land-based programs in a 100% beneficial way.
