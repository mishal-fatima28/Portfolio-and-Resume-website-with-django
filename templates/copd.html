What is Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD), it's symptoms and how physiotherapy can help in this regard?

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is a lung disease. A group of progressive diseases that cause obstructive airflow to and from lung leading to breathing issues is called COPD. The most common forms involved in COPD are ‘emphysema’ and ‘chronic bronchitis’. They cause coughing with mucus and air sac damage respectively. Smoking, gases exposure, fumes exposure and pollution are main causes of COPD. It develops slowly showing symptoms in person above 40 years of age. If complicated may cause lung cancer or heart problems.



Symptoms of Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD):

  • Breathing Problem
  • Coughing
  • Mucous Production with Cough
  • Weight Reduction
  • Wheezing with Breathing
  • Swollen Legs Or Feet


How Physiotherapy helps to cure Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD):


It has no cure, but the focus is to slow down the disease development. The physiotherapy can reduce risk of diseases by improving lifestyle. The ‘pulmonary rehabilitation program’ carried out by doctors and physiotherapists with ‘controlled oxygen therapy’ are very effective to control COPD. The breathing exercises, proper ventilation exercises, airway clearance techniques, and mobilization therapy are foremost requirements to recover COPD and provided by a physiotherapist at the palm of your hand. Medication is run along-with physical therapy.
