What is De Quervain Syndrome, it's symptoms and how physiotherapy can help in this regard?

The temporary inflammation of tendons and their sheath that controls movement of thumb is called as De Quervain syndrome. A painful sensation is felt on outside of wrist that increase with rotation of wrist. The exact cause is unknown but arthritis, inflammation, wrist injury or repeated movement of hand such as playing golf, lifting baby or garden work may lead to this injury. If not treated it may become permanent that lead to bursting of tendons. It is most common in women, and people with 30-50 years of age.



Symptoms of De Quervain Syndrome:

  • Wrist pain
  • Pain on thumb side
  • Swelling near thumb base
  • Sticking sensation in wrist when moved
  • Difficulty in thumb movement
  • Recovery period may be longer than 6 weeks.


How Physiotherapy helps to cure De Quervain Syndrome:


The recovery time is 4-6 months. Physiotherapist after assessment goes for therapy plan that includes application of physical agents, exercises to reduce thumb irritation. Limited exercise is allowed for few early weeks, followed by strengthening exercises during recovery time. If it goes for corticosteroid injections or surgery in severe disease, post-surgery physical therapy will help strengthen your motion with flexibility to work as normal. Manual therapy techniques include:

  • Hand movement
  • Soft tissue technique for muscle relaxation
  • Gliding techniques
