What is Groin injuries, it's symptoms and how physiotherapy can help in this regard?

A groin strain is overstretching or tearing injury to the muscles of the internal thigh or front of the hip. Groin strains make strolling, lifting the knee, or movement of leg very difficult and irritated. Acute groin strains can happen from abuse of the muscles, or from an abrupt constriction of the muscles. It might take 4 to about a month and a half, yet that is only an unpleasant gauge. Individuals recover at various rates.


Athletes involved in ice hockey and soccer possess high risk of injury than other athletes. Groin strains aren't generally genuine, in spite of the fact that an extreme strain may set aside a long effort to recover from. The time that you need to recover will likewise rely upon your degree of wellness before the injury.


Symptoms of Groin injuries:

  • Pain usually felt in the inner thigh
  • Decreased strength in the upper leg
  • Swelling
  • Bruising
  • Difficulty walking or running without pain
  • Snapping sound at the moment of injury
